Room-Specific Supply Air Solution

Room-specific ventilation differs from central ventilation in so that each room that is air-conditioned has a separate device that can be used and adjusted per room. No ducting is required. We specialize in these systems.

Supply air valves are required to bring supply air into the house or a building. Even during the cold winter times, pre-heating Mobair supply air valves can bring the supply air inside without the feeling of draught, with good filtration and very low energy consumption.

Low energy consumption

Energy consumption is the lowest in room-specific ventilation. Central air conditioning, even with heat recovery, consumes more energy because it does not take into account the stay in the rooms and the whole house air is exchanged, including empty rooms. For room ventilation, air is exchanged only in rooms where there are people, generally 2-3 rooms.

Low cost & maintenace

The cost of room-specific ventilation is only a fraction of the cost of a central system. On average, 2-4 supply air valves are needed, which cost less than €1000 in total. Central ventilation with ducting costs around €8000. A further advantage is that the cleaning and maintenance of supply air devices is considerably easier than the conventional supply air duct.

Uncontrolled vs. pre-heated inflow of cold air

A balance is achieved between the temperatures of the lower and the upper areas of the room when the supply air is pre-heated. When the cold supply air flows indoors through the walls, windows frames or the foundations of the building, it cools down the floor surface creating the feeling of draught.
